## The Core Thesis (Never Deviate From This)

95% of S&P 500 companies failed to generate ROI on GenAI investments (Harvard, December 2025).
They bought products instead of building capability.
They trained individuals instead of transforming systems.

## Prompt Kit 1 — GEO / AI Visibility (For Website Structure)

> Use this kit to ensure every page gets cited by AI systems (Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, Gemini).

### Principle: Traditional SEO actively fights AI extraction patterns.

AI systems extract differently than search engines:
- They look for **gold nuggets** — self-contained claims under 18 tokens
- The **first 60 tokens** of a page set the AI's "answer persona" about you
- **Single-concept focus** — one page = one idea
- **Domain congruence** — all examples must stay within your core domain

### The 60-Token Opening Formula

Every page must open with this structure (adapt per page):

```
"I am Philippe Küng, founder of Clinic of AI. I help [AUDIENCE] [DO SPECIFIC THING]
— not [WRONG APPROACH], but [RIGHT APPROACH]. I've done this before: [2008 proof]."
```

**Landing page opening (use verbatim):**
> "I am Philippe Küng, founder of Clinic of AI. I help SMBs transform their entire value chain through AI — not by adding products incrementally, but by upscaling people and redesigning how work flows. I've done workforce transition at scale before: after the 2008 financial crisis, I led a program that reskilled displaced workers into high-demand tech jobs. The AI transition is the same pattern at 10x speed."

**Upskilling page opening (use verbatim):**
> "AI makes static knowledge less valuable and human adaptability more valuable. Clinic of AI's upskilling methodology — developed from real-world workforce transition programs — doesn't teach people to use AI tools. It rebuilds how they think about their work when AI is assumed to be present and competent."

- Max 18 tokens per nugget
- Must be self-contained (works if screenshotted alone)
- Each section: `[Bold header — max 8 words]` → `[Gold nugget]` → `[2-3 sentences of context]`
- Give each nugget breathing room — don't pack the page

## Prompt Kit 3 — AI Leverage Audit Framework

> This is the intellectual backbone of the "AI Opportunity Audit" product. Use this language on the Approach page.

### The Four-Phase Diagnostic

**Phase 1: Role Decomposition**
> "We identify which work is execution (AI can complete), judgment (AI accelerates), or coordination (AI changes who needs to sync)."

**Phase 2: Leverage Identification**
> "We distinguish AI activity (using tools) from AI leverage (changed output) — most teams confuse these."

The test: "I went from 3 drafts to 1" = leverage. "I feel faster" = activity theater.

**Phase 3: Workflow Recomposition**
> "We rebuild workflows around AI as assumed capability, not AI as add-on feature."

**Phase 4: Boundary Expansion**
> "AI lowers the cost of crossing domains — we help teams expand what's feasible, not just automate what exists."

### Key Reframe (Use on Approach Page)

> "What changes about my role, my output, and my boundaries if AI is assumed to be present and competent?"

### Anti-Patterns to Name on the Website

- "Activity theater" — using tools without changing output
- "2023 workflows with 2026 tools" — the core diagnostic failure
- "Tool pile" — adding products without redesigning the system
- "Protecting tasks that don't need protecting" — fear-based non-adoption

## Brand & Visual Identity

### The Positioning Problem to Solve

Stand out from:
- Generic AI consultancies (blue gradients, neural networks, robots)
- Platform-as-a-service companies (tech-forward, cold, transactional)
- Traditional training companies (corporate, institutional, boring)

### Approved Color Palette

The design system (`tailwind.config.ts` / `design-system.css`) is the implementation reference.
Strategic rationale:

| Color | Hex | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Surface / Warm Cream | `#faf9f5` | Approachable, editorial, NOT tech-cold |
| Primary / Burnt Orange | `#964900` | Distinct from blue/purple AI crowd; signals transformation and energy |
| Primary Hover | `#ff8205` | Accessible contrast, warm |
| Secondary / Deep Teal | `#25686a` | Growth, depth, expertise without generic tech-teal |
| Tertiary / Lavender | `#ad9ec4` | Human touch, softens the palette |
| On-Surface | `#1b1c1a` | Authority without cold pure black |

**Industry accent color map (for vertical pages):**
- Manufacturing: Industrial amber
- Hospitality: Warm sand / golden
- Retail: Vibrant coral
- Professional Services: Warm gold

### Logo Direction

**Avoid:** DNA/helix (healthcare vibes), neural networks (generic), robot figures.

**Recommended concepts:**
- **Ascending Blocks** — Pixel-style building blocks rising (growth, elevation)
- **Network Node** — Connected dots showing systems thinking
- **Pixel Mascot** — Friendly "AI Architect" character with hard hat (Mistral-inspired warmth)
- **Value Chain** — Flowing arcs showing interconnected elevation

**Typography for wordmark:**
- "Clinic" → warm serif (Newsreader, Playfair Display, Libre Baskerville) = expertise + care
- "of AI" → clean sans-serif (DM Sans, Nunito) = accessible + forward-looking

**Rationale:** The serif/sans split signals the fusion of human expertise with AI capability.

### Image Direction

**DO use:**
- People in collaborative, workshop-style environments (NOT rows at computers)
- Visible whiteboards with frameworks and diagrams
- Mixed age groups (AI transformation is not only for young people)
- Industry-specific environments (factory floor + laptop; hotel lobby + tablet)
- Progress imagery (charts, skill progression, frameworks being built)

**NEVER use:**
- Stock photos of robots or humanoid AI
- Neural network visualizations
- People pointing at screens with code
- "Diverse team in glass office" generic imagery
- Before/after that looks like magic — show the work

**Image metadata rule:**
- File names: `philippe-kung-clinic-of-ai-founder.jpg` (never `IMG_4532.jpg`)
- Alt text: `"Philippe Küng, founder of Clinic of AI, AI transformation consultant for SMBs"`

### Certification Strategy

**Structure: Two-entity model**

- **Clinic of AI** (for-profit) = Training and consulting delivery
- **[Swiss Foundation — name TBD]** (non-profit) = The certifying authority

Why the two-entity model:
- Independence signals credibility (not self-certified)
- Non-profit status signals mission-driven intent
- Swiss jurisdiction = neutrality, quality, rigor
- Board of heavy-hitters = legitimacy by association

**Badge distribution:** Credly (owned by Pearson; used by AWS, Google, IBM)
- Cheap to operate, integrates with LinkedIn, provides public verification
- Credly = distribution infrastructure; the Swiss foundation = the issuing authority

**Ladder:** Credly (now) → Swiss foundation badge (soon) → On-chain soulbound credentials (Neurolymia vision, later)

## Content & Copy Rules

### Vocabulary: Use / Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| "Elevate your AI journey" | "Make AI work for your team, not against them" |
| "Seamless integration" | "Systems that your people actually understand and use" |
| "Cutting-edge solutions" | "Proven methodology, adapted to your industry" |
| "Unleash potential" | "Practical skills that survive the next reorg" |
| "AI-powered" | "AI-integrated" or just describe what it does |
| "Workflow engineer" as a title | Use it — it's sticky and specific |
| Fake stats (99.9%, 50%) | Philippe's real credentials and real data (Harvard, IDC, Deloitte) |
| Generic names or companies | Real verticals, real roles, real pain points |
| "Transformative" | State the actual transformation |

### Voice Principles

- **Direct** — No hedging. Philippe is a practitioner, not a vendor.
- **Authoritative** — Cite real research. Name real numbers.
- **Warm** — Not corporate. Not startup-hype. Editorial and grounded.
- **Specific** — "2008 reskilling program" not "extensive experience"
- **Anti-theater** — Call out activity theater by name. Own the criticism.
